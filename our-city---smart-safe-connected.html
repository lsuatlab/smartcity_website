OUR CITY

The goal of this S&CC planning proposal is to build a partnership between various Baton Rouge community stakeholders and a multidisciplinary team of academic researchers in order to develop research concepts that will promote and employ S&CC technologies to tackle the major factors affecting the economic progress of the region, by considering economic and social issues in a holistic manner.

This visualization was created by Jacob Shelton, an undergraduate REU student from Washington State University. Created from three years of crime data hosted on Open Data BR, this visualization aggregates the data into a crime index based on whether or not the crime was committed and the type of crime attempted. Next, Baton Rouge is divided up based on police district location data from EBRGIS Open Data and the crime index is used to color the police district. This visualization is not meant to convene any conclusive findings as the data from Open Data BR is meant only to give a snapshot of criminal activity of any given district. Instead this visualization serves as an example for one visualization style offered by the Smart City team.
